Frequently Asked Questions

Are 10 minute mail alternatives legal?

Yes, all reputable 10 minute mail alternatives are completely legal. They're legitimate privacy tools. However, using them to violate a website's terms of service or engage in fraudulent activity is prohibited. Always use temporary email responsibly and ethically.

Why do some websites block 10 minute mail services?

Websites block temporary email services to prevent spam accounts, ensure user accountability, and maintain database quality. Some services with paid subscriptions also want to ensure users provide real contact information. This is why having multiple alternatives with different domains is helpful.

Can I use 10 minute mail alternatives for important accounts?

No, temporary email services should never be used for important accounts like banking, healthcare, primary social media, or anything requiring password recovery. These services are designed for temporary, non-critical use only. Use your permanent email for accounts you need long-term access to.
