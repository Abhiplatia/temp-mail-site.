What Are Gmail Aliases?

Before comparing temp mail vs Gmail aliases, understand what Gmail aliases are. Gmail offers two aliasing methods: Plus addressing (adding "+anything" before @gmail.com, like user+shopping@gmail.com) and dot notation (Gmail ignores dots, so u.s.e.r@gmail.com reaches user@gmail.com). Both deliver to your primary inbox while appearing as different addresses externally. Aliases help organize incoming mail through filters but remain permanently linked to your real email account.

What Is Temporary Email?

In the temp mail vs Gmail aliases comparison, temporary email provides completely separate addresses unconnected to your real identity. Services generate random addresses (like abc123@tempmail.com) that exist temporarily—often 10 minutes to 24 hours. Emails arrive at a disposable inbox accessible via web browser. After expiration, the address and all messages disappear permanently. No connection exists between your temporary address and real email accounts.
